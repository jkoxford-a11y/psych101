## Fig. 4.1 — Sensation vs. Perception: From Energy to Meaning

**Files:** fig_4_1_sensation_perception_pipeline.png, fig_4_1_sensation_perception_pipeline.svg

## Fig. 4.3 — The Retina Is Not a Camera Sensor

**Files:** fig_4_3_retina_rods_cones_fovea_blindspot.png, fig_4_3_retina_rods_cones_fovea_blindspot.svg

**Caption:** Rods, cones, the fovea, and the blind spot show why visual sensation is already biologically organized before perception begins.

**Alt text:** Labeled eye cross-section showing light entering the pupil, focusing on the retina, with cones concentrated at the fovea, rods more common in the periphery, and a blind spot where the optic nerve exits.

## Fig. 4.9 — Gate Control Theory of Pain

**Files:** fig_4_9_gate_control_pain.png, fig_4_9_gate_control_pain.svg

**Caption:** Pain is modulated at the spinal cord by incoming touch signals and descending signals from the brain.

**Alt text:** Diagram showing injury signals and touch signals entering the spinal cord, a modulatable gate, upward pain transmission to the brain, and descending brain signals that can partly close the gate.
