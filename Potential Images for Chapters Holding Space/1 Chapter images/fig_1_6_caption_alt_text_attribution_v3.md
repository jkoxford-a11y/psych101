# Figure 1.6 — Three Ways to Explain Fluent Language, v3

**Suggested filename:** `fig_1_6_language_explanations_skinner_chomsky_llm_v3.png`

**Caption:**  
Figure 1.6. Three ways to explain fluent language. Skinner’s behaviorist account emphasized learned verbal behavior shaped by consequences. Chomsky argued that language requires generative internal structure capable of producing and understanding novel sentences. Large language models complicate the debate by producing fluent language through statistical learning over massive text corpora. The shared teaching point is that fluent output alone does not settle what mechanism produced it.

**Alt text:**  
Three-panel diagram comparing explanations of fluent language. The Skinner panel shows a cue-response-outcome loop and states that consequences shape future verbal behavior. The Chomsky panel shows a simple grammar tree and states that internal structure makes novel language possible. The large language model panel shows text corpus to learned weights to next-token prediction to fluent answer, and states that fluent text can emerge from learned statistical patterns. A bottom note says that output alone does not settle mechanism.
