## Fig 1.1 — History of Psychology as a Problem Chain
Filename: fig_1_1_history_problem_chain.png / .svg
Caption: Major schools of psychology can be understood as a sequence of problem-solving moves. Each school answered a question that earlier approaches could not, but each also had limits that pushed the field toward a new approach.
Alt text: A horizontal flowchart shows structuralism/early lab psychology, functionalism, behaviorism, psychoanalysis, humanistic psychology, cognitive psychology, and biological/evolutionary psychology. Each box lists the school’s core question and its main limitation.

## Fig 1.2 — Seven Perspectives, One Behavior
Filename: fig_1_2_seven_perspectives_phone.png / .svg
Caption: The same behavior can be explained from multiple perspectives. Repeated phone checking can be understood biologically, behaviorally, cognitively, psychodynamically, humanistically, socioculturally, and evolutionarily.
Alt text: A central circle labeled “Repeatedly checking your phone” is surrounded by seven boxes, each naming a psychological perspective and a short explanation.

## Fig 1.3 — The Biopsychosocial Model
Filename: fig_1_3_biopsychosocial_triangle.png / .svg
Caption: The biopsychosocial model explains behavior and mental states as the interaction of biological, psychological, and social/environmental factors.
Alt text: A triangle connects biological factors, psychological factors, and social/environmental factors. Arrows from all three point toward an example outcome, depressed mood.

## Fig 1.4 — Common Sense vs. Scientific Psychology
Filename: fig_1_4_common_sense_vs_science.png / .svg
Caption: Common sense often moves from intuition to hindsight and confirmation bias. Scientific psychology uses hypotheses, systematic observation, controls, replication, and revision to make errors easier to detect.
Alt text: A two-column diagram contrasts a common-sense pathway with a scientific pathway. The common-sense side ends in false confidence; the scientific side ends in replication and revision.

## Fig 1.5 — Psychology Is Broader Than Therapy
Filename: fig_1_5_psychology_scope_map.png / .svg
Caption: Psychology is broader than therapy. The field includes clinical and counseling psychology, cognitive psychology, biological psychology, developmental psychology, social psychology, educational psychology, industrial-organizational psychology, forensic psychology, health psychology, human factors, comparative psychology, and performance psychology.
Alt text: A radial map places psychology in the center and shows multiple subfields around it, emphasizing that therapy is only one part of the discipline.
