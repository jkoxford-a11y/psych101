## Figure 4.3 - The Retina Is Not a Camera Sensor

**Files:** `fig4_3_retina_cutaway.png`, `fig4_3_retina_cutaway.svg`, `fig4_3_retina_cutaway.pptx`

**Caption:** Rods and cones are not evenly distributed across the retina. Cones are densest at the fovea, supporting color and fine detail; rods dominate the periphery, supporting dim-light sensitivity. The blind spot, where the optic nerve exits, contains no photoreceptors.

**Alt text:** Simplified eye cross-section with a zoomed retina inset showing the fovea, rods, cones, optic nerve, and blind spot. Cones are labeled as concentrated at the fovea, rods as more common in the periphery, and the blind spot as lacking photoreceptors.
