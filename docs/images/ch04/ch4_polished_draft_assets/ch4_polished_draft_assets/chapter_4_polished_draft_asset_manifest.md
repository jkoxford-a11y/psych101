## Gate Control Theory of Pain

**Slug:** `gate_control_pain_modulation`

**File:** `gate_control_pain_modulation.png`

**Caption:** Gate control theory proposes that pain is not a direct wire from tissue damage to conscious experience. Nociceptor input, touch input, and descending signals from the brain interact at the spinal cord to modulate how much pain signal reaches awareness.

**Alt text:** Educational infographic showing pain signals from nociceptors, non-pain touch signals, descending control from the brain, and modulation in the dorsal horn of the spinal cord before pain signals ascend to the brain.

**Attribution:** AI-generated draft instructional image created for Jon Oxford’s General Psychology textbook project. Based conceptually on Melzack and Wall’s gate control theory; final labels should be reviewed before public release.

## Pitch Is Coded in Two Ways

**Slug:** `cochlea_place_frequency_pitch_coding`

**File:** `cochlea_place_frequency_pitch_coding.png`

**Caption:** The cochlea encodes pitch through complementary mechanisms. Place theory explains how pitch is represented by where the basilar membrane vibrates most, while frequency theory explains how lower pitches can be represented by the firing rate of auditory neurons.

**Alt text:** Educational infographic showing a cutaway cochlea, an unrolled basilar membrane with high-frequency peaks near the base and low-frequency peaks near the apex, and waveform examples showing slower and faster neural firing.
