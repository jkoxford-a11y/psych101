## Instructional purpose

Show one common transmission sequence followed by two alternative clearance routes:

1. Signal transmission: action potential arrival, neurotransmitter release, receptor binding, and changed ion flow.
2. Clearance route A: reuptake through a transporter in the presynaptic membrane.
3. Clearance route B: enzymatic breakdown of unbound neurotransmitter in the synaptic cleft.

The three-panel structure is intended to prevent students from reading reuptake and enzymatic degradation as consecutive stages of one mandatory pathway.

## Caption

**Synaptic transmission begins when an action potential triggers neurotransmitter release from the presynaptic terminal. Neurotransmitter molecules cross the synaptic cleft and bind to postsynaptic receptors, changing ion flow and the receiving neuron’s likelihood of firing. Unbound neurotransmitter is then cleared by different mechanisms. Some molecules are taken back up through transporters in the presynaptic membrane; others are broken down by enzymes in the synaptic cleft. Reuptake and enzymatic degradation are alternative clearance routes, not required consecutive steps.**

## Alt text

Three-panel educational infographic explaining chemical synaptic transmission and two clearance routes. The first panel shows an action potential reaching a blue presynaptic terminal, neurotransmitter molecules being released into the synaptic cleft, binding to receptors on a green postsynaptic neuron, and changing sodium or potassium ion flow. The second panel shows unbound neurotransmitter moving through a reuptake transporter in the presynaptic membrane and back into the terminal. The third panel shows unbound neurotransmitter in the synaptic cleft being broken into inactive fragments by an enzyme. The final two panels present reuptake and enzymatic breakdown as alternative ways to end the signal.

## Accuracy constraints

- Synaptic vesicles remain inside the presynaptic terminal.
- Reuptake transporters are embedded in the presynaptic membrane.
- Receptors and ion channels are embedded in the postsynaptic membrane.
- Enzymatic breakdown occurs in the synaptic cleft.
- Panel 1 contains no enzyme and no reuptake process.
- Panel 2A demonstrates reuptake only.
- Panel 2B demonstrates enzymatic breakdown only.
- Panels 2A and 2B are alternative clearance routes, not consecutive required stages.
- The figure should not imply that all neurotransmitters use the same clearance mechanism.
- The figure should not imply that transmitter binding is always excitatory.
- Do not add calcium channels, SSRIs, or an embedded figure number.
