## Core teaching purpose

This figure shows the zone of proximal development as the band between what the learner can already do independently and what is not yet reachable. It also shows the mechanism of scaffolding: support is initially calibrated to make the next step possible, then gradually withdrawn as competence becomes independent.

## Caption

Vygotsky's zone of proximal development is the range of tasks a learner cannot yet complete alone but can complete with well-calibrated support from a more capable partner. Scaffolding works only when it targets that middle zone: too little help changes nothing, while too much help completes the task without building independent competence.

## Alt text

A two-part diagram explains Vygotsky's zone of proximal development. On the left, nested circles show what a learner can do alone in the center, what can be done with support in the middle ring, and what is not yet reachable in the outer ring. On the right, a three-step sequence shows scaffolding being gradually reduced until the learner can perform independently.
