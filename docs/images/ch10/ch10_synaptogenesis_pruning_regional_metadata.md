# ch10_synaptogenesis_pruning_regional.svg

## Caption
Early brain development overproduces synapses beyond adult levels, then prunes unused ones back: use it or lose it. Sensory regions such as visual and auditory cortex peak and settle earlier, while the prefrontal cortex peaks later and keeps pruning into adolescence. Curves are schematic, illustrating the Huttenlocher pattern rather than a single dataset.

## Alt text
A line chart plots relative synaptic density against age from birth to adulthood for visual cortex, auditory cortex, and prefrontal cortex. All three curves rise above a dashed adult-level reference line and then decline toward it. Visual and auditory cortex peak early and settle earlier; prefrontal cortex peaks later and declines more gradually into adolescence, which is marked with a shaded band.
