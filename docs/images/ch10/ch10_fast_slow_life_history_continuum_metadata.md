# ch10_fast_slow_life_history_continuum.png

## Caption
Every species sits somewhere on a fast-to-slow life-history continuum: a trade-off, not a ranking. Fast species reproduce early and often, with many offspring and little investment in each. Slow species reproduce late and rarely, investing heavily in a few. Humans sit at the extreme slow end, with the longest childhood of any animal and cooperative care around a single dependent child.

## Alt text
A horizontal infographic arranges animals from fast life history on the left to slow life history on the right. A mouse with many pups, a rabbit with several kits, a deer with one fawn, an elephant with one calf, and a human child with an adult and an older helper appear along the axis. Labels contrast reproducing early versus late, many versus few offspring, and little versus heavy investment.
