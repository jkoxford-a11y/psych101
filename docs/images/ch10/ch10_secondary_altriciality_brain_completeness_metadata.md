## Caption
Human infants are born with brains at only about 28% of eventual adult volume, less complete than a chimpanzee infant's brain, which is closer to 40%. Being born less finished is not a deficit: it means more of the brain is built after birth, where experience can shape it. Maximum immaturity at birth buys maximum postnatal plasticity.

## Alt text
Two circular indicators compare brain completeness at birth. The human infant indicator is filled to about 28% of adult brain volume, while the chimpanzee infant indicator is filled to about 40%. Labels say humans build more after birth and are more shaped by experience, while chimpanzees build more before birth.
