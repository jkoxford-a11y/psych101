# ch10_attachment_styles_reunion_matrix.svg

## Caption
Ainsworth's attachment patterns are distinguished less by distress at separation than by behavior when the caregiver returns. Secure infants seek comfort and are soothed; anxious-ambivalent infants both seek and resist comfort; anxious-avoidant infants suppress attachment behavior and appear indifferent. A fourth disorganized pattern sits outside the core three. These are relationship patterns, not fixed destinies.

## Alt text
A matrix compares attachment styles. Secure infants seek comfort, are soothed, and return to exploring; anxious-ambivalent infants seek and resist comfort and are hard to soothe; anxious-avoidant infants avoid the caregiver and show little visible distress. Columns also list typical caregiving history and approximate shares in Western samples. A separate bottom row notes disorganized attachment.
