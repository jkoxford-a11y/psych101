# ch10_cross_sectional_vs_longitudinal.svg

## Caption
Developmental change can be studied with cross-sectional or longitudinal designs. A cross-sectional study compares people of different ages at one time, which is quick but confounds age with cohort. A longitudinal study follows the same people across years, capturing within-person change but taking longer and losing participants over time.

## Alt text
Two panels compare research designs. The cross-sectional panel shows separate groups in their 20s, 50s, and 80s all tested in 2026, with a note that age is tangled with cohort. The longitudinal panel shows the same people followed from 2026 to 2041 to 2056, with a note that the design is slow and participants drop out.
