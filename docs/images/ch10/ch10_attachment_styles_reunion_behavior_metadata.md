## Core teaching idea

Ainsworth's attachment classifications are distinguished most clearly by infant behavior at reunion, not by separation distress alone. Secure, anxious-ambivalent, anxious-avoidant, and disorganized patterns should be interpreted as relationship patterns in a caregiving context, not as fixed destinies or simple labels attached to the child.

## Caption

Attachment classifications in the Strange Situation are read most clearly at reunion. Secure infants seek comfort and are readily soothed; anxious-ambivalent infants both seek and resist comfort and are harder to settle; anxious-avoidant infants suppress attachment behavior and appear distant; disorganized infants show contradictory or frightened behavior. These are probabilistic relationship patterns, not fixed destinies, and prevalence estimates vary across studies and cultures.
