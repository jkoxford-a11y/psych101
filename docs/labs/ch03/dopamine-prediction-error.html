    3. What the Pattern Means

    Concept Reveal

    Dopamine-related reward-prediction signals are strongest when outcomes differ from expectations. After learning, the predictive cue carries information that used to arrive with the reward. The psychological meaning depends on pathway, target circuit, receptor context, and current task—not on the chemical alone.

    This visualization uses normalized teaching-model signal units, not measured dopamine firing rates or concentrations. The classical reward-prediction-error model is powerful but incomplete: different dopamine pathways also contribute to movement, salience, novelty, learning, and action vigor.
