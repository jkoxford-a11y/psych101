Learning Lab - Chapter 3

Chemical Imbalance Claim-Check

Brain chemistry matters. The misconception is the simpler claim that a disorder is caused by one missing chemical and that a helpful medication proves that original cause. In this 10–20 minute lab, you will classify claims, explain the causal-inference error, and transfer the reasoning to a new example.

3. What the Pattern Means

Concept Reveal

A treatment effect shows that changing a system can change symptoms. It does not, by itself, prove that the disorder began as a deficiency in that system.

The careful correction has two parts: simple one-chemical deficiency stories are not established general causes, and brain chemistry can still participate in symptoms and treatment through receptors, circuits, development, body state, and environment. Rejecting an oversimplification is not the same as rejecting biology.
