Learning Lab - Chapter 9

Finding the Rule: Fluid Intelligence as Model-Building

Fluid intelligence is easiest to understand when you have to use it. In this lab, you will infer rules from unfamiliar visual patterns, apply those rules to missing cases, and notice how errors happen when the wrong feature grabs your attention.

Accessibility and Privacy

This lab runs entirely in your browser. It stores your prediction, puzzle progress, explanation, and transfer response in sessionStorage for this browser session only. It does not collect names, emails, IDs, analytics, or personally identifying information.

Core Idea

Fluid intelligence

Reasoning through a novel problem when the rule is not already stored in memory.

Compressed rule

A simpler pattern extracted from several examples, such as "shape changes across rows."

Blind spot

A distracting feature can pull attention away from the rule that actually predicts the answer.

    What the puzzles just showed you

    These puzzles did not ask you to recall a fact. They asked you to compress visible examples into a rule, then apply that rule to a new case. That is the chapter's core idea for fluid intelligence: building a useful model from unfamiliar material under constraint.

    Instructor Note

    Use this lab before the intelligence section. Have students commit predictions, solve the first two puzzles individually, then compare strategies in pairs before the distractor puzzle.
