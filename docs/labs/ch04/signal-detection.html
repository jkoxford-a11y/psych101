Signal Detection: Sensitivity Is Not Criterion

Question: Can the same sensory evidence produce different decisions when the cost of a miss changes? Plan on 12–15 minutes.

Accessibility and privacy

This visual demonstration stores your prediction, trial progress, explanation, and transfer response only in sessionStorage for this browser tab. It collects no identifying information or analytics and sends nothing to a server. Because detecting a faint visual target is the phenomenon being demonstrated, the visual trials have no equivalent nonvisual sensory test. The outcome table, event log, and written scenarios still provide access to the decision concepts without claiming the same sensory experience.

    What the pattern means

    A more liberal criterion means less evidence is needed before saying “present.” That usually captures more real signals and accepts more noise as signal. The sensory display did not improve; the decision rule changed.

    This is a teaching demonstration, not a calibrated measure of sensory ability. Monitor characteristics, viewing distance, fatigue, and the small number of trials all matter.
