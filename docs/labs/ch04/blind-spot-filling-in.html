Blind Spot and Perceptual Filling-In

Question: What do you experience where the retina supplies no input at all? Plan on 8–12 minutes.

Accessibility and privacy

This phenomenon inherently depends on vision and steady fixation. The instructions and mechanism are fully described in text, but there is no nonvisual substitute for personally experiencing retinal filling-in. 4. Observe and explain

Why there is a blind spot

The optic nerve exits through the optic disc, a patch of retina with no rods or cones. When an image falls there, no receptor signal describes that location. Perception normally supplies a plausible continuation from nearby information; it does not recover the objectively correct missing content. Your other eye usually covers the missing region too, which is why ordinary viewing hides the gap so well.
