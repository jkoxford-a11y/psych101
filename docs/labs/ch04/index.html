Learning Labs - Chapter 4

Sensation and Perception

These activities ask what your senses detect, what your decision rules add, and how context changes the experience built from the same input. Work through one lab at a time; each keeps responses only in the current browser session.

Detection and thresholds

Signal detection theory

Sensitivity Is Not Criterion

About 12–15 minutes. Classify noisy trials under two decision policies and observe hits, misses, false alarms, and correct rejections. No materials required.

Weber's Law

Difference Depends on Proportion

About 8–12 minutes. Compare line lengths while absolute and proportional changes are varied. No materials required.

Perceptual construction

Blind spot and filling-in

What Replaces Missing Input?

About 8–12 minutes. Move a target and a patterned gap while testing each eye separately. No materials beyond the screen; the phenomenon depends on vision.

Top-down influence

Context Changes Perception

About 8–10 minutes. Interpret an original ambiguous glyph in letter and number contexts, then compare the physically identical input. No materials required; a text alternative is included.

Motion and organization

Existing demonstration

Heider–Simmel Social Motion

About 5 minutes. Observe moving geometric shapes and notice how quickly perception supplies goals and social meaning. No physical materials required.
