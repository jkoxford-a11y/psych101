Why Does “About Me” Stick?

You are about to make two kinds of meaningful judgments about trait words. Some judgments ask what the word means in general. Others ask whether the word could describe you in at least some situations. After a brief interruption, you will compare what you remember.

This is a memory demonstration, not a personality test, diagnostic assessment, or controlled experiment on you. A short individual result can differ from the usual group-level pattern by chance.

Accessibility and Privacy

      What the activity was designed to show

      Both conditions required semantic processing. Self-reference added a connection to a large, familiar, and organized body of knowledge: what you know about yourself. Psychologists call that organized knowledge a self-schema. Connecting a word to the self-schema can elaborate it and create additional retrieval cues. Across groups, information processed in relation to the self is often remembered better than information given a general semantic judgment. This pattern is called the self-reference effect.

      Do Not Confuse: A Memory Effect with a Personality Test

      The activity does not infer anything about your personality from the traits you endorsed. It also cannot determine why self-reference sometimes improves memory. Researchers have proposed elaboration, organization, and the exceptional familiarity of self-knowledge as overlapping explanations. A single short demonstration cannot distinguish among them or prove that the self is a unique memory system.

      Instructor Note

      Use this after the levels-of-processing lab or as a separate demonstration of elaboration and self-schema. Compare the class-level pattern rather than treating one student's result as decisive. The key debrief question is: if both conditions required meaning, what additional cognitive work did self-reference require?

      Evidence Base

      The design adapts the trait-judgment logic introduced by Rogers, Kuiper, and Kirker (1977) and the broader self-reference-effect literature. Later syntheses support a reliable memory advantage while leaving room for several overlapping mechanisms, especially elaboration and organization.
