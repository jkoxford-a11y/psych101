General Psychology

Learning Labs

Learning Labs are short interactive exercises for practicing key psychology concepts. Each lab asks you to make a prediction, commit to an answer, observe more evidence, explain your reasoning, and transfer the idea to a new case.

Chapter 2 - Research Methods & Statistics

Correlation vs. Causation Classifier

Classify fictional research headlines, reveal the study design, and decide whether the evidence supports a causal claim.

Chapter 3 - Neuroscience & Biological Bases

Action Potential Threshold and All-or-Nothing

Test subthreshold and suprathreshold inputs, then compare spike height with firing rate under sustained stimulation.

Chapter 3 - Neuroscience & Biological Bases

Dopamine Prediction Error

Predict cue and reward responses across unexpected reward, learning, omission, and extinction trials.

Chapter 3 - Neuroscience & Biological Bases

Chemical Imbalance Claim-Check

Classify brain-chemistry and medication claims as supported, overstated, or wrong / incomplete.

Chapter 4 - Sensation & Perception

Sensation & Perception Learning Labs

Four guided labs on signal detection, Weber's Law, blind-spot filling-in, and context-sensitive perception, plus the existing Heider–Simmel social-motion demonstration.

Chapter 5 - Consciousness

What Does the Evidence Actually Show?

Classify what findings from blindsight, attention, hallucination, drugs, and AI directly demonstrate—and where an explanation goes beyond the evidence.

Chapter 6 - Sleep

Sleep Learning Labs

Separate homeostatic sleep pressure from circadian timing, then use converging physiological evidence to distinguish sleep stages and parasomnias.

Chapter 7 - Learning

Classical Conditioning Simulator

Run acquisition, extinction, and spontaneous recovery trials while tracking conditioned response strength.

Chapter 8 - Memory

What Makes a Word Stick?

Process words by appearance, sound, or meaning, then compare which encoding judgment produced the strongest later recognition.

Chapter 8 - Memory

Why Does “About Me” Stick?

Compare general semantic judgments with self-reference judgments, then test whether linking information to the self changed later recognition.

Chapter 8 - Memory

Can You Make Two Words Stick Together?

Move from a natural study strategy to interactive imagery and then bizarre interaction while testing cued recall across three rounds.

Chapter 9 - Thinking, Language & Intelligence

Finding the Rule: Fluid Intelligence as Model-Building

Infer rules from unfamiliar visual patterns, apply them to missing cases, and distinguish fluid intelligence from crystallized knowledge.

Chapter 10 - Lifespan Development

Lifespan Development Learning Lab

Compare complete-answer support with graduated hints, then test what remains after support fades on a related task.

Chapter 11 - Social Psychology

The Feed Is Not the World

Rate your view on a low-stakes campus issue, scroll a simulated feed that adapts to your own clicks, and see how perceived consensus and confidence shift without any new evidence.
