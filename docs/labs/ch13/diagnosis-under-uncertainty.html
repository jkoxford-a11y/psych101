Learning Lab - Chapter 13

Diagnosis Under Uncertainty

A symptom description is the beginning of clinical reasoning, not the end. In this lab, you will update an evidential judgment as information arrives.

Allow about 10–15 minutes. All people and situations are fictional composites. You are classifying the strength of the evidence, not diagnosing a real person.

Privacy and Boundaries

This lab runs entirely in your browser. It stores only your prediction, progress, evidence judgments, explanation, and transfer response in sessionStorage for this browser session. It stores no names, emails, IDs, personal health information, analytics, or tracking data, and nothing is sent to a server. Do not enter information about yourself or another real person.

    What the Sequence Shows

    Classification under uncertainty

    Early symptoms can justify concern without supporting a diagnosis. Duration, impairment, context, associated symptoms, medical and substance exclusions, and differential evidence change the strength of the inference. Even after a diagnosis is supported, the label does not establish cause or select one treatment automatically.
